Our story

Twelve years of
the same question.

“Will it still look like this in ten years?” That is the question we ask about every material we buy and every joint we cut. It is why we moved to marine ply carcasses before it was common here, and why we would rather lose a job than quote a number we cannot honour.

We started in a small unit off the Khaira road in Bhawarna, taking on kitchens that larger studios found too far out. Word travelled through the valley the way it does here — one family to the next. Two hundred homes later we still work the same way: measure it ourselves, cut it ourselves, fit it ourselves.

We are not the cheapest quote you will get, and we are honest about that. What we offer instead is a number that does not move, a team that turns up, and a phone that still gets answered three years after handover.

What we hold to

Four things we will not
trade away.

Honest quoting

Line-by-line pricing before you commit. If something is going to cost more, you hear it during design — not after installation has started.

Material over margin

Marine ply where moisture reaches, real hardware that survives a decade of daily use. We would rather explain a higher price than defend a failure.

Our own hands

Employed carpenters and fitters, not day labour hired per site. The person who measured your kitchen is usually there when it goes in.

Service that lasts

Ten years on the carcass, and a working phone number after that. Local business, local reputation — we cannot afford to disappear.

Where we work

Across the valley.
